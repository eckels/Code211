What is Code211?

Code211 is a 20 hour coding event spanning over 2 days where students from across the district will converge at Palatine High School to create amazing projects and learn about Computer Science. At the event, we will be having beginner to advance workshops and teams can compete against each other to win prizes that are judged by industry professionals.

FAQ's

See some of the most asked questions about our event

Who can participate?

Anyone as long as you go to school in District 211!

How much does it cost?

Nothing! Everything is free of charge including food and t-shirts.

What should I bring?

A laptop, charger, and any other amenities you need. The event is not overnight so you will not need any sleeping items. If you don't have a laptop, we will have a limited number onsite.

Will there be prizes?

Yes! Stay tuned for the prize distribution and categories.

What can I create?

Anything as long as it is school appropriate. You have full liberty to make games, apps, or websites. We encourage you to create projects that have positive impact. If you are struggling to think of ideas, visit chicagohacks.org to see winning projects and to explore another great hackathon.

What type of workshops will be there?

We will have workshops ranging from building your first website to machine learning. All workshops will be on Friday and Saturday.
